Junior Career and Senior Career of McKayla Maroney

Junior career

She started her junior career in 2009 participating in Visa Championships in Dallas, Texas. At the time, she was only 13 years 8 months old. She finished twenty-seventh in the all-around and third in the vault final. She also participated at Cover Girl Classic Competition, Visa Championships, and Pan American Championships, all in 2010. At the 2010 Pan  American Championships, she contributed American team to finish in the first place, as she also finished first on vault and floor.

Senior Career

Following her outstanding performance during her junior career, she started her senior career in 2011 when she was only 16 years 10 months old. She competed at the 2011 World Artistic Gymnastics Championships in Japan. Team American finished in the first place, where she contributed a vault score of 16,033 and a floor score of 14,566. She also won the gold medal in the vault final with a score of 15,300.

She was selected in the American team, “Fab Five” for the 2012 Summer Olympics in London. She contributed her team to finish first and she clinched the silver medal in the vault final. She is credited to change the team’s nickname from the “Fab Five” to “Fierce Five”. She achieved a success in a short period of time due to her hard work, practice, and dedication.

Her photograph of pursed lips on the medal podium after winning a silver medal in the vault in London Olympics became an internet phenomenon. Then President of the United States also posed the pursed lips with her when team America met the U.S President, following their recent Success.

Following, her success at the 2012 London Olympics, she suffered a leg injury, which kept her out of competitive gymnastic almost for 8 months. She won another gold medal at the 2013 World Artistic Gymnastics Championships in Belgium, becoming the first U.S female gymnast to defend a World

Artistic Gymnastic Championships vault title.

Following knee surgery in March 2014, and underage nude image leak in August 2014, she retired from competitive gymnastics in 2016. She also claimed to have struggled with adrenal fatigue after the 2013 World Championships. She made her acting debut in Series Hart of Dixit (2012-2015) in 2012, she also appeared on an episode of the Fox TV show, Bones in 2013, and Superstore in 2016.
