Will Yun Lee Biography

Will Yun Lee (born Lee Sang-Wook) is an American actor and martial artist. He is best known for his roles as Danny Woo in the supernatural drama Witchblade and Jae Kim in the sci-fi series Bionic Woman. He has also appeared in the films Die Another Day (2002), Elektra (2005), and The Wolverine (2013). He had a recurring role as Sang Min in Hawaii Five-0, plays the original body of series protagonist Takeshi Kovacs in Altered Carbon, and voiced Wei Shen in the game Sleeping Dogs (2012). As of 2019, he is appearing on the ABC medical drama The Good Doctor playing Dr. Alex Park.

Will Yun Lee Career

Lee appeared in films such as Die Another Day, Torque, The King of Fighters, and Elektra as a supporting actor. He has also appeared in the FX Network mini-series Thief and the ABC Family TV movie Mini-series Fallen. He recently appeared as a recurring character “Sang Min” on Hawaii Five-0 and as “Mr. Gus” on True Blood.

In James Mangold’s The Wolverine, he also played Kenuichio Harada (2013). Lee appeared in several episodes of the Netflix science-fiction series Altered Carbon in 2018 as the original body of protagonist Takeshi Kovacs (who was otherwise primarily played by Joel Kinnaman in season one and Anthony Mackie in season two).

As undercover cop Wei Shen, Lee provides the voice of the main protagonist in Square Enix’s Hong Kong open-world crime game Sleeping Dogs. People named him one of the “50 Most Beautiful People” in 2002, which led to a string of high-profile roles.

In November 2007, he was named to People’s list of the 15 “Sexiest Men Alive” for the second time. Lee is one of the actors, producers, and directors interviewed in Jeff Adachi’s 2006 documentary The Slanted Screen, which examines the portrayal of Asian and Asian American men in Hollywood. He appeared in Mariah Carey’s “Boy (I Need You)” and Ice Cube’s “Roll Call” music videos.
